Audio samples from "Improving Fine-grained Emotion Control and Transfer with Gated Emotion Representations in Speech Synthesis"

Abstract: Fine-grained emotion strength control and prediction is recently studied in text-to-speech to adjust local emotion intensity in an utterance. Due to the lack of fine-grained emotion strength labelling data, emotion or style strength extractor is usually learned at the whole utterance scale through a ranking function. However, such utterance-based extractor is then used to provide fine-grained emotion strength labels, conditioning on which a fine-grained emotional speech synthesis acoustic model is independently trained. To bridge the granularity gap between the emotion strength extractor learning and the emotional speech synthesis acoustic model training, a simple yet effective component called Emotion Gate is introduced to directly learn fine-grained emotion strengths in an end-to-end paradigm. Furthermore, beside predicting from a jointly trained emotion strength predictor, proposed method also allows to manually assign and control the fine-grained emotion strengths during inference. In experiment part, proposed method is evaluated in both non-transferred emotional speech synthesis and cross-speaker transferred scenarios. Both objective and subjective evaluations show the effectiveness and superiority of proposed method over the state-of-the-art baseline systems.
